Juice

Juice was Tupac Shakur acting debut playing Quicy "Q" Powell. Juice is a 1992 American crime thriller film directed by Ernest R. Dickerson, and written by Dickerson and Gerard Brown. It also stars Omar Epps,Jermaine Hopkins and Khalil Kain. The film touches on the lives of four black youths growing up in Harlem, following their day-to-day activities, their struggles with police harassment, rival neighborhood gangs and their families.

Poetic Justice

Poetic Justice is a 1993 cult classic American romantic drama film written and directed by John Singleton. Tupac Shakur plays Lucky a young postal clerk who falls in love with Justice who is played by Janet Jackson. In the film, the main character Justice writes poems which she recites throughout the film that were written by Maya Angelou.

Above The Rim

Above the Rim is a 1994 American sports film co-written and directed by Jeff Pollack. The film tells about a promising New York City high school basketball star and his relationships with a drug dealer and a former basketball star, now employed as a security guard at the high school. Tupac Shakur stars as Birdie, the local drug dealer and the film's antagonist.

Gang Related

Gang Related is a 1997 American crime thriller film written and directed by Jim Kouf. The film revolves around two corrupt cops who attempt to frame a homeless man for the murder of an undercover DEA agent they themselves had killed. Tupac Shakur plays Detective Jacob "Jake" Rodriguez who is one of the detectives that gun down a narcotics dealer, after they engage illegally in drug trafficking.The film, Shakur's last, was released over a year after his death.
